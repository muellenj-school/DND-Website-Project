How to Homebrew

What is Homebrewing?

Homebrewing is taking the original features and mechanics of Dungeons & Dragons and making them your own.

Common Types of Homebrew

One of the most common types of homebrew content includes spells, objects, and actions. These require careful design and balancing to improve gameplay without breaking it. Even small changes can have a big impact.

Another popular form is creating full campaigns. This includes building original enemies, locations, and characters to create a unique world.

Advanced Homebrew

Some creators go further by designing entirely new systems, essentially creating a brand-new tabletop game.

When building a campaign, focus on:

  • Strong worldbuilding
  • Engaging characters
  • Balanced encounters
  • A compelling main conflict
  • Meaningful player choices

Inspiration

Many homebrew worlds are inspired by existing ideas like sci-fi (cyberpunk), fantasy (like Lord of the Rings), modern settings, or historical eras.

You can create almost any world you imagine by changing races, settings, or even the rules of the game itself.

Common Homebrew Elements

  • Tools/Objects
  • Characters with full stat sheets
  • If rebuilding it, make your own leveling system
  • Classes and Races
  • Most Importantly: A WORLD
